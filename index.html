Editor in WebGL

A small series of demos that take a real, functioning text editor — input, selection, undo, IME and all — and render its picture through a layer of WebGL. The editor brain is always a hidden CodeMirror instance; only what you see in front of it changes.

  1. 01

    Undulating surface

    Dark brown text on a tan page that bends and swells under a domain-warp shader. A serif you can write on like cloth.

  2. 02

    Writing in clouds

    Thick sans-serif glyphs lit from within, raymarched as a volumetric slab of noisy smoke against a dark sky.

  3. 03

    Letters from stones

    Every glyph reassembled out of small 3D pebbles, instanced from the rasterised silhouette of the live document.

Each demo is a standalone folder containing an index.html, script.js, styles.css and a README.md walkthrough. No build step; open any index.html directly or serve the repo with python3 -m http.server.
